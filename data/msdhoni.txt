MS Dhoni (Mahendra Singh Dhoni) is a former Indian cricketer and captain of the Indian national team, widely regarded as one of the greatest finishers in limited-overs cricket. He played as a wicketkeeper-batsman and was known for his calm demeanor, strategic acumen, and ability to perform under pressure.

Dhoni led India to numerous major victories, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. He is celebrated for his helicopter shot, sharp wicketkeeping skills, and his leadership, which helped nurture young talents in Indian cricket.

Post-retirement, Dhoni continues to influence cricket as a mentor and captain in domestic leagues and is admired for his contributions to Indian sports and leadership both on and off the field.
